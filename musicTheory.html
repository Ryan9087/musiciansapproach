The Modes of the Major Scale

Musical modes are a type of scale with distinct melodic characteristics. The 7 modes, Ionian, Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Aeolian and Locrian, come from the earliest forms of western music. Before we figured out the math for dividing the octave into 12 equal tones, we had to make do with an imperfect system. Modes were the solution. Instead of one all-purpose scale that could be transposed into different keys, there were 7 modes that each had their own structure. Today, modal music refers to pieces of music where modes are used structurally and harmonically in place of traditional functional harmony.
